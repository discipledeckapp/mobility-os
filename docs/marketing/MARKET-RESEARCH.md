## 7. Regulatory Framework

| Regulation | Country | Effective | Biometric Classification | Penalty |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| NDPA 2023 (Nigeria Data Protection Act) | Nigeria | 2023 | Sensitive personal data — requires explicit consent + DPIA | Up to 2% of annual gross revenue or NGN 10M (whichever is higher) |
| Data Protection Act 2019 | Kenya | 2021 (phased) | Special category data | KES 5M or 1% of annual turnover |
| Data Protection Act 2012 (Act 843) | Ghana | 2012 | Sensitive data | GHS 60,000 + criminal penalties |
| POPIA (Protection of Personal Information Act) | South Africa | Fully enforced 2021 | Special personal information | ZAR 10M or 10 years imprisonment |

**Regulators:**
- Nigeria: Nigeria Data Protection Commission (NDPC) — successor to NITDA
- Kenya: Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC)
- Ghana: Data Protection Commission
- South Africa: Information Regulator

**Transport-specific:**
- Nigeria: FRSC mandate for digital driver records (ongoing)
- Lagos State: Digital commercial vehicle registration push (LAMATA / LASG)
- Kenya: NTSA TIMS system for vehicle and driver digital records
- Ghana: DVLA + GPRTU registration requirements

## 8. Mobile and Connectivity

| Country | Smartphone Penetration | Dominant OS | Mobile Money |
|---|---|---|---|
| Nigeria | 45–55% | Android 95%+ | OPay, PalmPay growing; legacy bank apps |
| Ghana | 50–60% | Android 93%+ | MTN MoMo dominant (~60% adult adoption) |
| Kenya | 55–65% | Android 90%+ | M-Pesa dominant (~80% adult adoption) |
| South Africa | 75–85% | Android 85%+ | Bank-led; higher bank penetration than mobile money |

**Data costs (approx.):**
- Nigeria: $1–3/GB
- Kenya: $0.50–1.50/GB (more competitive)
- Ghana: $1–2/GB

**Implication for product:** Offline-first mobile design is not optional for field workers. Low-end Android (sub-$150 device) must be the design target for the mobile app.

**Sources to verify:** GSMA Mobile Economy Sub-Saharan Africa report (annual, free), Statista country mobile data.
