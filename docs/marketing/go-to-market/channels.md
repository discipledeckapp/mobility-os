# Acquisition Channels

---

## Channel Strategy

Mobiris serves a B2B operator segment that does not use Google to find software, does not read tech press, and makes buying decisions through trusted relationships and peer networks. The channel strategy has to reflect that.

**Principle:** Get in front of fleet managers through the channels they already trust — their unions, their peers, their vehicle suppliers, and their financiers.

---

## Primary Channels

### 1. Direct / Founder-Led Outreach

**Stage:** Now → first 50 customers

The founding team reaches out directly to fleet operators through personal networks, referrals, and LinkedIn. This is the only channel with enough signal quality in the early stage to learn whether the pitch is landing and why prospects do or don't convert.

**Execution:**
- Build a target list of 100 operators in Lagos, Abuja, Nairobi, Accra — sourced from NURTW/GPRTU/MOAK directories, LinkedIn, and Uber/Bolt fleet partner communities
- Personalise outreach to the specific pain visible from outside (e.g., "We saw your fleet is operating on Bolt — we help fleet partners manage driver compliance and documentation")
- Lead with a problem, not a product demo
- Offer a 30-min walkthrough + 2-week hands-on trial for qualified operators
- Target conversion: 1 paying customer per 10 qualified conversations

**Owner:** Founder / first sales hire

---

### 2. Transport Union and Association Partnerships

**Stage:** Month 3 → ongoing

Transport associations are the trust layer for this market. An endorsement from NURTW (Nigeria), GPRTU (Ghana), or MOAK (Kenya) opens doors that direct outreach cannot.

**Key targets:**

| Association | Country | Membership | Approach |
|---|---|---|---|
| NURTW (National Union of Road Transport Workers) | Nigeria | Millions of drivers and owners | Operator-level members are the buyer; leadership buy-in accelerates trust |
| RTEAN (Road Transport Employers Association) | Nigeria | Operators (owners) specifically | Closer ICP alignment than NURTW |
| GPRTU (Ghana Private Road Transport Union) | Ghana | Trotro and bus operators | Entry point for Accra/Kumasi |
| MOAK (Matatu Owners Association of Kenya) | Kenya | Matatu owners, SACCOs | SACCO leaders are key influencers |
| SANTACO (SA National Taxi Council) | South Africa | Taxi associations | Entry point but highly political — tread carefully |

**Tactics:**
- Sponsor or present at annual general meetings / expos
- Run workshops: "How to verify drivers and track remittance digitally" — education-first, no hard sell
- Offer association members a discounted entry plan (co-branded where possible)
- Propose a pilot with 5–10 association member operators at subsidized cost

---

### 3. Fleet Financing Partner Channel (Moove, Local Lenders)

**Stage:** Month 6 → scalable channel

**Moove** is the dominant vehicle financing player for ride-hail fleet operators in Nigeria, Ghana, and Kenya. They have financed thousands of vehicles. Every Moove operator needs a management system — and Moove has an interest in their operators running well (it reduces default risk).

**Why this works:** Moove and similar financiers want their financed operators to be measurably compliant. Mobiris provides that audit trail. This is a mutual interest, not a favour.

**Approach:**
- Explore a co-marketing or integration partnership with Moove
- Propose a data-sharing arrangement where Mobiris operational data (assignment completion, remittance rates) is a signal for loan health (with operator consent)
- Similar approach with microfinance banks that lend to fleet operators (LAPO, AB Microfinance, etc.)
- Fleet vehicle dealers (Coscharis, CFAO) as referral partners — they know which operators are buying vehicles and expanding

**Target:** 20% of growth-stage pipeline from partner channel

---

### 4. LinkedIn (Founder Account — Organic)

**Stage:** Now → ongoing

Decision-makers in this segment — fleet managers, MDs of transport companies, operations directors — are increasingly on LinkedIn, especially in Nigeria. Founder-led thought leadership builds credibility before a sales call.

**Content pillars:**
- Operational war stories ("How one Lagos operator lost ₦2M/month in untracked remittance — and what changed")
- Market insight ("How many commercial vehicles are in Lagos? The answer surprised us")
- Product perspective ("Why we built driver self-service — and why guarantors are the overlooked risk")
- Regulatory pulse ("What NDPA means for transport operators who store driver biometrics")

**Frequency:** 3x/week. Alternate pillars. No consecutive product posts.

**Secondary:** Company page posts of case studies, product updates, and market data.

---

### 5. WhatsApp and Community Distribution

**Stage:** Month 2 → ongoing

The ICP uses WhatsApp as their primary business communication channel. Fleet operator communities, NURTW chapter groups, and ride-hail fleet partner communities all exist on WhatsApp.

**Tactics:**
- Identify and join relevant WhatsApp groups (where operator communities share news, discuss problems)
- Share genuinely useful content (compliance guides, remittance tracking templates) before pitching
- Enable referral virality through WhatsApp: "Share this link with another fleet operator, you both get one month free"
- Customer success interactions happen on WhatsApp — this is where African B2B support actually lives

## Secondary / Experimental Channels

| Channel | Hypothesis | When to Test |
|---|---|---|
| **Uber/Bolt fleet partner communities** | Fleet partners have driver compliance pain; Mobiris fits their compliance requirement | Month 4+ |
| **Fleet insurance bundling** | Insurers (Leadway, AXA Mansard, Old Mutual) want fleets to be compliant; could bundle Mobiris as a condition | Month 9+ |
| **Commercial vehicle dealers** | Coscharis, CFAO know who is buying fleets; referral arrangement | Month 6+ |
| **Accounting software referrals** | Operators using BukkHQ or Zoho for accounting may need Mobiris for the operational layer | Month 9+ |
| **Google Search / SEO** | Long-term organic; build authority on "fleet management Nigeria", "driver verification Nigeria" | Month 6–12 horizon |
| **YouTube demos** | Practical "how to manage remittance" content for operators who research on YouTube (common in Nigeria) | Month 6+ |

## Channel Anti-Patterns

- **Broad Meta/Google Ads**: Fleet managers don't click ads for business software. Wrong discovery behaviour for this segment.
- **Product Hunt / HackerNews**: Wrong audience entirely.
- **Cold email at volume**: Low deliverability, wrong channel for operators. LinkedIn DM or WhatsApp is better.
- **Press / Tech Media (TechCabal, Disrupt Africa) too early**: Wait until you have a customer story. Press without proof accelerates nothing.

---

## Retention as the Primary Growth Channel

In this market, **word of mouth within operator networks is the strongest channel** — and it's gated by the product actually solving the problem. A single satisfied fleet manager at a NURTW chapter can refer 5–10 peers. Invest in customer success before scaling acquisition.

Key retention levers:
- White-glove onboarding for the first 20 customers
- WhatsApp support (operators do not use email for support)
- Regular check-in calls in the first 90 days
- Feature input sessions: let operators see that their feedback ships
