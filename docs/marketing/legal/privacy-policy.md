## 5. Biometric Data

This section applies specifically to the facial biometric data processed during Driver and Guarantor verification.

### 5.1 What We Process

We conduct biometric liveness verification to confirm that a Driver or Guarantor is a real, live person submitting their own biometrics. This involves:

- Capture of a facial image or video frame during a liveness session
- Transmission to AWS Rekognition or Microsoft Azure Face for liveness analysis
- Generation of a mathematical facial **embedding** (a numeric vector representing facial geometry)

**We do not retain the raw facial image or video after the liveness verification is complete.** Only the encrypted embedding is stored.

### 5.2 Why We Process Biometric Data

The lawful basis for processing biometric data is **explicit consent**, obtained from the Driver or Guarantor at the start of the self-service verification flow. The consent screen clearly states:

- What biometric data will be captured
- That raw images are not retained
- That an encrypted embedding will be stored
- The purpose (identity verification and cross-operator fraud detection)
- The right to withdraw consent (which will result in the Driver not being verified on the platform)

Operators must not initiate biometric verification for any Driver or Guarantor who has not provided informed consent.

### 5.3 How Biometric Data Is Protected

- Biometric embeddings are encrypted at rest using AES-256 encryption
- A unique per-record encryption key is derived from a master key stored in a secure secrets manager
- Embeddings are stored in a segregated database schema accessible only to the Intelligence Plane service
- No Operator can access the raw biometric embeddings of their Drivers — only the verification status and risk signals
- Access to the embedding data is restricted to the platform's Intelligence Plane service and authorised engineering personnel for maintenance purposes only

### 5.4 Biometric Data Retention

Biometric embeddings are retained for as long as the Driver record is active on the platform, plus 12 months after the Driver record is deleted or deactivated, to support any regulatory or legal enquiry during that period. After this, embeddings are permanently deleted.

### 5.5 NDPA Classification

Biometric data is classified as **sensitive personal data** under Section 30 of the Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023. We conduct and maintain a Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) for our biometric processing activities and will make a summary available to Operators on request.

## 7. The Intelligence Plane and Cross-Tenant Data

The Intelligence Plane is a cross-operator risk system. To be transparent about how it works:

- Every Driver verified on the platform has an anonymised risk profile maintained in the Intelligence Plane
- This profile does not contain the Driver's name, employer name, or any Operator-specific records
- If a risk signal is present (from a prior fraud flag, watchlist entry, or identity inconsistency), it is surfaced as a **risk score** to the Operator conducting the new verification
- No Operator can see the identity of the flagging Operator or the underlying incident records
- The processing lawful basis for cross-operator risk signals is **explicit consent** (obtained during Driver onboarding) and **legitimate interests** (fraud prevention in the transport sector)

Drivers may request a copy of any risk signal associated with their identity and request correction if it is inaccurate. See Section 11 (Your Rights).

## 9. International Data Transfers

Some of our sub-processors are located outside Nigeria. Where we transfer personal data internationally, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place, including:

- Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) with AWS and Microsoft Azure
- Data Processing Agreements with all sub-processors that include transfer safeguards
- Assessment of the data protection laws of the destination country

Biometric liveness processing by AWS Rekognition involves transfer to the United States. This processing is time-limited (the result is returned and the raw image discarded); the embedding is stored in Nigeria on our primary database hosted via Render.

## 12. Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data, including:

- TLS encryption for all data in transit
- AES-256 encryption for biometric embeddings at rest
- Segregated database schemas per platform plane (control, operational, intelligence)
- Role-based access controls and least-privilege principles
- Regular security reviews
- Separate JWT secrets per service boundary
- No raw biometric images retained post-verification

In the event of a personal data breach that is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify the Nigeria Data Protection Commission (NDPC) within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach and notify affected individuals without undue delay, in accordance with the NDPA 2023.
