# Positioning

## Category

**Mobility Risk Infrastructure for vehicle-for-hire operators in Africa.**

Mobiris is not a generic logistics platform, a telematics tracker, a fleet management dashboard, or a fintech product. It is the risk infrastructure layer for transport companies that need to verify who is behind the wheel, enforce remittance accountability, and surface cross-operator fraud signals — with a complete audit trail.

## Market Context

The African fleet management market was valued at approximately **$2–3 billion in 2023** and is growing at **15–20% CAGR**, projected to reach $5–7 billion by 2030. But the telematics-heavy products dominating that number serve corporate fleets and trucking companies. They do not serve the segment that represents the actual majority of vehicles on African roads.

**The vehicle-for-hire segment is the biggest, least-served market in African transport:**

| Country | Est. Commercial Vehicles | Informal Transport Share | SaaS Adoption |
|---|---|---|---|
| Nigeria | 5–7 million | ~90% of urban trips | <1% |
| South Africa | 3–4 million | ~60% of trips | ~5% (most mature) |
| Kenya | 1.5–2 million | ~80% of trips | <2% |
| Ghana | 800K–1.2 million | ~85% of trips | <1% |

These operators — running danfo buses in Lagos, matatus in Nairobi, trotros in Accra, minibus taxis in Johannesburg — generate **$15–60/day per vehicle in remittance**, employ an estimated **10+ million people** across the four target countries, and run almost entirely on paper and WhatsApp.

## Problem Statement

Vehicle-for-hire operators run complex, cash-heavy operations with no fit-for-purpose software:

1. **Driver management is manual** — no biometric verification, no document system, no cross-operator fraud signals. A driver who absconded with a vehicle at one company walks into the next one and gets hired.

2. **Remittance is untracked** — daily cash collection averaging ₦7,500–₦25,000 per vehicle with no ledger, no dispute trail, no reconciliation. Operators estimate 10–20% leakage from unrecorded shortfalls.

3. **Fleet assignment is informal** — WhatsApp dispatching with no completion records, no audit trail, and no way to hold a driver accountable for an assignment they claim was cancelled.

4. **Compliance is a liability** — expired licences, unverified guarantors, no documentation for regulators. NDPA (Nigeria), Kenya DPA, and equivalent regulations increasingly require biometric data to be handled with consent and audit trails.

## Regulatory Tailwind

All four target markets have enacted or strengthened data protection legislation:

- **Nigeria** — NDPA 2023 (replacing NDPR). Biometric data is sensitive personal data requiring explicit consent and DPIA. Fines up to 2% of annual gross revenue.
- **Kenya** — Data Protection Act 2019 (GDPR-modeled). Special category data rules apply to biometrics.
- **Ghana** — Data Protection Act 2012 (Act 843). Controller registration required.
- **South Africa** — POPIA. Fully enforced. Most mature framework.

Simultaneously, governments are pushing to **formalize informal transport**: LASG digital vehicle registration drives in Lagos, NTSA's TIMS system in Kenya, GPRTU requirements in Ghana. Operators who want to comply need a system of record. Mobiris is that system.

## What We Are Not Positioning Against

- Consumer ride-hailing apps (Uber, Bolt, InDrive) — different buyer, different problem
- Telematics / GPS tracking (Ctrack, MiX, Geotab) — complementary, not competing; they track the vehicle, we manage the operator-driver-remittance relationship
- Ride-hail fleet financing (Moove) — adjacent; Moove operators are potential Mobiris customers
