# Competitive Positioning

Last updated: March 2026

---

## The Competitive Landscape

There is no purpose-built competitor for the vehicle-for-hire fleet operations segment in Africa. This is both the market opportunity and the main sales challenge — operators aren't looking for software because no one has told them software exists for their specific problem.

The competitive set is indirect. These are the products operators use as workarounds, or products that serve adjacent segments.

---

## Competitor Categories

### Category 1 — Telematics / GPS Tracking

**Named competitors:** MiX Telematics (South Africa HQ, pan-African), Ctrack (acquired by Verizon Connect, dominant in SA), Geotab (resellers in Africa), Samsara (US-focused, minimal Africa presence)

**What they sell:** Real-time vehicle location tracking, mileage reports, engine diagnostics, driver behaviour scoring (via OBD or dashcam).

**Pricing:** $20–45/vehicle/month plus hardware ($100–500/vehicle for the tracking unit). MiX Telematics is the most established in Africa; Samsara prices are out of range for most African SME operators.

**Why operators use them:** Insurance requirements in South Africa and Kenya sometimes mandate telematics. Some logistics companies use them for route optimisation.

**Why they don't solve the fleet operator's actual problem:**
- Track the vehicle, not the driver-remittance relationship
- No driver identity verification
- No remittance lifecycle
- No guarantor management
- No cross-operator fraud signals
- Hardware cost is prohibitive for small operators
- Built for corporate fleets and logistics, not vehicle-for-hire

**Win against them:** "You have a GPS tracker. That tells you where your vehicle is. Mobiris tells you who's driving it, whether they paid today, and whether you can trust them in the first place. Those are different problems."

**Coexistence note:** Telematics and Mobiris are complementary. Telematics tracks the vehicle; Mobiris manages the operator-driver relationship. This is a potential partnership angle with MiX or Ctrack — refer each other's products.

---

### Category 2 — Logistics / Freight SaaS

**Named competitors:** Kobo360 (Nigeria, freight marketplace), Lori Systems (Kenya, logistics), Sendy (Kenya, last-mile delivery), SWVL (Egypt/pan-African, pivoted to B2B)

**What they sell:** Freight matching, last-mile delivery orchestration, route optimisation for goods transport.

**Why they don't compete:**
- Focus is on moving cargo, not managing a driver remittance relationship
- Their driver is a "trip provider" not a daily remittee
- No guarantor management, no remittance ledger, no vehicle-for-hire fleet hierarchy
- Kobo360 raised $30M+ and is focused on enterprise trucking — not SME passenger/minibus operators

**Win against them:** Different category. These platforms serve shippers and logistics companies. Mobiris serves the operator who assigns drivers to vehicles and collects daily remittance.

---

### Category 3 — Ride-Hail / Fleet Financing Platforms

**Named competitors:** Moove (pan-African vehicle financing, Nigeria/Ghana/Kenya/Egypt/SA), Treepz / Plentywaka (corporate shuttle Nigeria), Shuttlers (corporate commute Nigeria)

**Moove** is the most interesting case. They finance vehicles for ride-hail fleet operators on Uber/Bolt, charging a weekly repayment deducted from driver earnings. They have financed thousands of vehicles and have deep relationships with exactly the operator profile that is Mobiris's ICP.

**Why they don't compete:**
- Moove provides financing, not operations software. Moove operators still have no management platform.
- Treepz/Shuttlers serve the corporate shuttle segment — different remittance model, B2B buyer.

**Partnership opportunity:**
Moove-financed operators need a management platform. Mobiris operational data (assignment completion rates, remittance consistency) is a natural loan health signal for Moove. A co-marketing or integration arrangement with Moove could be the fastest enterprise channel to unlock.

**Win against them (Treepz/Shuttlers):** Different buyer. But if a Treepz prospect is also running a separate owner-operated fleet, they need Mobiris for the non-corporate portion.

---

### Category 4 — Generic ERP / Business Software

**Named competitors:** Odoo, Sage (SME), QuickBooks, SAP (enterprise), BukkHQ (Nigerian accounting SaaS)

**What operators use them for:** Accounting, payroll, basic inventory management.

**Why they don't solve the fleet operator's problem:**
- No driver verification or identity management
- No remittance lifecycle (confirm, dispute, waive)
- No assignment dispatch
- No guarantor management
- No cross-operator intelligence
- Implementation cost is high (Odoo requires setup from a partner)
- Not built for the African vehicle-for-hire operating model

**Pricing reference:** Odoo SaaS starts at $9.10/user/month but adds quickly with modules. BukkHQ Nigerian accounting is $10–30/month. These are the benchmarks operators will compare against.

**Win against them:** "Can your accounting software tell you which of your drivers hasn't paid remittance in three days? Or flag a driver who has a fraud history at another company? That's what Mobiris does — it's not accounting, it's fleet operations."

---

### Category 5 — Manual Process (The Real Competition)

**The "product":** WhatsApp groups, paper notebooks, Excel/Google Sheets, oral agreements.

**Why operators use it:** Free. Familiar. Requires no change management. Adequate for very small fleets.

**Why it breaks:**
- At 20+ vehicles, reconciling daily remittance from a WhatsApp group takes 1–2 hours per day
- No audit trail — disputes have no resolution
- A fraudulent driver moves freely between operators — no shared record
- Compliance documentation doesn't exist — a bank or regulator request is a crisis
- Scale is impossible — you can't franchise or grow beyond what the owner can personally track

**Cost of the status quo:**
- Remittance leakage: estimated 10–20% of collections per month
- For a 50-vehicle operator at ₦10,000/day/vehicle: ₦1.5M–₦3M/month in leakage
- Mobiris Growth at 50 vehicles: ~₦80,000/month

**Win against them:** Don't pitch software. Ask them to calculate their leakage. "How much do you think you're losing to untracked remittance every month? What would half of that be worth?" The math makes the case. Then show the product.

## Positioning Phrases (Quick Reference)

| When prospect mentions... | Response |
|---|---|
| "We use spreadsheets / notebook" | "How long does it take to reconcile 30 drivers' remittance in the morning? What happens when someone disputes a payment?" |
| "We already have a tracker (GPS)" | "That's tracking the vehicle. Mobiris manages the relationship — who's driving it, what they paid today, and whether you can trust them." |
| "We use QuickBooks / BukkHQ" | "Great for accounting. Does it tell you which drivers haven't paid this week, or flag a driver with fraud history at another company?" |
| "Moove manages our vehicles" | "Moove finances the vehicles — who manages your driver records, remittance tracking, and compliance? That's what Mobiris does." |
| "We have our own system (built internally)" | "What does it do for driver verification and cross-operator risk signals? Those are the hardest parts to build correctly." |
| "It's expensive" | "What are you losing to untracked remittance every month? Mobiris at 50 vehicles costs less than 1 day of a conservative leakage estimate." |
