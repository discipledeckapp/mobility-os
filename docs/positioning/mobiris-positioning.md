## Category

**Mobility Risk Infrastructure**

Mobiris is not a fleet management platform. It is not a logistics dashboard. It is not a telematics tool. It is the risk infrastructure layer that transport companies run on — the system that verifies who is behind the wheel, enforces remittance accountability, and surfaces cross-operator fraud signals that no single operator can see alone.

The distinction matters. "Fleet management" implies a dashboard for visibility. "Risk infrastructure" means that without Mobiris, the operator is exposed to identity fraud, remittance leakage, and compliance liability. The product doesn't manage a fleet. It structures and de-risks how a transport company operates.

## One-Line Position

> Mobiris is the risk infrastructure that transport companies in Africa use to verify drivers, enforce remittance accountability, and eliminate cross-operator fraud — built for how the vehicle-for-hire segment actually works.

## Core Messaging Pillars

### 1. Verification
Know exactly who is behind your wheel — biometric liveness, government ID check, guarantor verification, document review. A driver who has not been verified should not be assigned. Mobiris enforces that.

### 2. Accountability
Every payment confirmed, disputed, or waived. An unresolved remittance shortfall is a compliance risk, not a conversation. Mobiris creates the permanent record that ends disputes.

### 3. Risk Intelligence
A driver who defrauded another operator won't slip through yours. The cross-operator identity graph surfaces risk signals — without exposing any operator's raw records to another. This only works at scale. This is the infrastructure layer no single operator can build.

### 4. Structural Compliance
Licence expiry alerts, version-tracked documents, audit trails, consent records. When a regulator, bank, or insurer asks for documentation, operators on Mobiris can produce it. Operators who are not on Mobiris cannot.

## Tone

- **Direct.** Say what the product does. Do not hedge.
- **Specific.** Use real numbers (₦7,500–₦25,000/day remittance, 10–20% leakage) when making claims.
- **Operator-first.** The buyer is a transport company owner or fleet manager. Write to them.
- **Not hype-heavy.** Do not use superlatives without evidence. Do not say "revolutionise" or "transform."
- **Not vague.** "Manage your fleet" says nothing. "Verify drivers before they get your keys" says something.

## Disallowed Language

These phrases misposition the product and must not appear in human-facing content:

| Disallowed | Why | Replace with |
|---|---|---|
| fleet management platform | Wrong category; implies telematics/GPS/dashboard | mobility risk infrastructure |
| driver management system | Passive; sounds like HR software | driver verification and control |
| logistics dashboard | Wrong segment; implies freight/B2B logistics | — |
| tracking tool | Implies passive GPS tracking | enforcement / accountability / monitoring |
| "track remittance" (as primary claim) | Passive; implies visibility only | "enforce remittance accountability" |
| "manage drivers" (as primary claim) | Passive; implies admin tool | "verify and control drivers" |
| "manage your fleet" (as primary claim) | Passive | "structure and control your fleet" |
| operations platform (as the primary description) | Generic SaaS category | secondary to risk infrastructure; acceptable in sub-copy |

**Exceptions:** Technical documentation, ADRs, and developer-facing code comments may use "fleet management" and similar terms as descriptive shorthand. The restriction applies to human-facing marketing and UI copy only.

## Audience-Specific Messaging

### For transport company owners / MDs
Lead with: financial exposure, fraud risk, compliance liability.
Avoid: product feature lists.
Use: "Your drivers are either verified or they're not. Mobiris makes sure they are."

### For fleet managers / heads of operations
Lead with: operational readiness, remittance accountability, assignment structure.
Use: specific daily workflows.
Avoid: infrastructure language (too abstract for day-to-day buyer).

### For investors / strategic partners
Lead with: category creation, infrastructure network effects, regulatory tailwind.
Use: "Mobility Risk Infrastructure" as the category.
Note: This is where "infrastructure" works best — convey that Mobiris is the system of record for a 10M+ vehicle segment, not just a dashboard.

### For drivers (self-service flows)
Lead with: simplicity and clarity. "Complete your verification."
Never expose: internal risk scores, compliance flags, fraud signals.
Language: Plain, clear, no jargon. "Your operator has invited you to complete your driver registration."
