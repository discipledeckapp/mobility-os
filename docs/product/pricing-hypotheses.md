# Pricing Hypotheses

## Purpose

Capture early pricing assumptions to be validated through customer discovery, competitive research, and commercial pilots.

## Initial Pricing Ideas

| Hypothesis | Model | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Base subscription per tenant | Flat monthly fee | To validate |
| Usage uplift by active asset count | Per vehicle / driver above base | To validate |
| Premium intelligence / risk scoring add-on | Per-query or monthly bundle | To validate |
| Enterprise control-plane premium | ARR deal with custom terms | To validate |

## Pricing Levers

1. **Per active vehicle** — aligns cost to value; scales naturally with fleet growth
2. **Per active driver** — alternative to vehicle pricing for driver-centric models
3. **Per verification / biometric call** — enables consumption-based Intelligence pricing
4. **Remittance volume band** — percentage cut on cash collected; aligns platform revenue to tenant revenue
5. **Flat seat licensing** — simple for SME customers; predictable for both sides

## Hypotheses to Validate

- [ ] What is a fleet operator's willingness-to-pay per vehicle per month?
- [ ] Is the Intelligence add-on perceived as high-value or a nice-to-have?
- [ ] Do enterprise customers prefer ARR or consumption billing?
- [ ] Does a free trial tier accelerate or dilute conversion?
- [ ] What remittance volume threshold makes the transaction-fee model attractive?

## Pricing Principles

1. Pricing must be derivable from the subscription plan and usage events — no manual invoice editing
2. Custom pricing must be encoded in the plan catalog, not in billing code
3. Trial periods are platform-wallet-funded (credits) — not freeware
