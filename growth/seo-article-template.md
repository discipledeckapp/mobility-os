## Structure

### 1. Headline

Use plain language.

Examples:

- `How to track driver remittance without losing visibility`
- `Why weak driver records create avoidable fleet losses`
- `A better way to manage vehicle assignments in a growing fleet`

### 2. Opening

Start with a real operational pain, not a definition.

Example frame:

`Many fleet operators do not have a software problem first. They have a visibility problem. They cannot clearly tell who had a vehicle, who has paid, who still owes, or which records can be trusted. That is where losses start.`

### 3. Pain section

- describe the real-world problem
- mention how it shows up day to day
- keep it concrete

### 4. Why it happens

- manual records
- weak handover discipline
- fragmented verification
- scattered payment tracking

### 5. What better control looks like

- clear driver records
- structured assignment logs
- visible remittance or repayment state
- easier follow-up and fewer disputes

### 6. Practical checklist

Use 4 to 7 bullets.

### 7. Soft product tie-in

Show how Mobiris fits without overselling.

### 8. CTA

Examples:

- `Book a quick demo`
- `Get the fleet control checklist`
- `Talk to us about your remittance workflow`
